03 — Business Model
FOUR REVENUE
STREAMS.

Sandlot's diversified model reduces risk and compounds growth. Membership creates recurring revenue. Institutional contracts create durable B2B revenue. Manufacturer partnerships make equipment free and generate co-marketing income. Data creates a long-term asset.

👤

Memberships

$19.99–$39.99/month individual. $4–6/student/month institutional. Recurring, subscription revenue with low churn (sport habit formation).

$96M ARR run rate at 1M members ($8 blended ARPU)
🏢

Institutional Contracts

Schools, cities, property managers, and corporations contract Sandlot as a service. Multi-year agreements. Low churn. Expands member base by thousands per deal.

Target: $5.4M ARR Year 3
🏭

Manufacturer Partnerships

Sports brands provide demo equipment at zero cost. In return, Sandlot delivers coupon activations, purchase data, and co-marketing reach. $1–3 per activation = $12–36M/yr at scale.

Target: $3.6M ARR Year 3
📊

Data & Wellness

1M active sports participants = a premium data asset. Health insurers, brands, and wellness platforms pay for access. FSA/HSA reimbursement integration adds B2B revenue channel.

Target: Long-term $10M+ opportunity
🎪

Per-Event Drop-In

Non-members pay per event. Casual users, corporate events, and special occasions. Converts to memberships at scale.

Target: $2.4M ARR Year 3
🏙️

City Manager Rev Share

Franchise-lite model. City Managers receive 8% of city revenue, accelerating expansion without full capital deployment from HQ.

Enables 50-city build without proportional HQ headcount
